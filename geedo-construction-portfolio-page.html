A landing page is any web page that a consumer can land on, but in the marketing realm, it's usually a standalone page, distinct from your homepage or any other page, that serves a single and focused purpose. A landing page is a follow-up to any promises that you've made in your content. Focus is why landing pages are so effective for marketing. As opposed to homepages and websites, which are designed for exploration, landing pages are customized to a specific campaign or offer and guide visitors towards a single call to action. In short, landing pages are designed for conversion. The landing page is a web page constructed to pull, and direct traffic that we can connect to your subdomain, check out the example below.

I constructed this survey landing page in order to pull traffic from users that normally engage with the social media content generated by the business's social media profile itself.
